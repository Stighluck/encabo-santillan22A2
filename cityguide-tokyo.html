TOKYO

Discover Tokyo, where ultramodern meets tradition in Japan's vibrant capital

Tokyo, Japan's vibrant capital, is a mesmerizing blend of ultramodern innovation and timeless tradition.

From its towering skyscrapers and cutting-edge technology to its serene temples and historic sites, Tokyo offers an unforgettable journey through contrasts and culture. The city is served by two major airports: Narita International Airport, located in Chiba Prefecture, and Haneda Airport, conveniently located just south of central Tokyo. These airports provide seamless connectivity, making it easy for travelers to reach this bustling metropolis.

Tokyo Weather

Tokyo has mild spring weather, followed by hot and humid summers punctuated with typhoons. Autumn in Tokyo is cool and fresh, followed by cold, dry winters. Snow is rare but has been known to fall in the first few months of the year. The one week forecast for Tokyo is an accurate guide to the weather for the coming week.

Top 6 Local Specialties

Sushi

Tokyo is renowned for its sushi, and it's a must-try for any visitor. From high-end sushi bars to conveyor belt sushi restaurants, you can find fresh and delicious sushi all over the city.

Tempura

Tempura, a dish of battered and deep-fried seafood and vegetables, is another Tokyo specialty. Asakusa is particularly famous for its tempura restaurants.

Yakitori

Yakitori, or grilled chicken skewers, is a popular street food and izakaya (Japanese pub) dish. Yakitori Alley near Yurakucho and Omoide Yokocho in Shinjuku are great places to enjoy this delicacy.

Ramen

Tokyo offers a wide variety of ramen styles, with famous areas like Shinjuku and Ikebukuro boasting numerous renowned ramen shops. Shoyu (soy sauce) ramen and miso ramen are particularly popular.

Monjayaki

Monjayaki is a type of savory pancake similar to okonomiyaki but with a runnier batter. Tsukishima is known as the birthplace of monjayaki and has many restaurants serving this unique dish.

Soba

Soba (buckwheat noodles) is another traditional Japanese dish that's widely enjoyed in Tokyo. You can find many soba restaurants offering both hot and cold versions of this noodle dish, often served with a variety of toppings.
