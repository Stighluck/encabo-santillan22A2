OKINAWA

Discover Okinawa: Where Culture, Sun, and Sea Unite!

Okinawa, Japan's Tropical Paradise. This tropical paradise, often referred to as the "Hawaii of Japan," is a haven for travelers seeking a diverse and unforgettable experience.

With its crystal-clear turquoise waters and pristine white sandy beaches, Okinawa is a dream destination for beach lovers. Snorkeling, diving, and swimming in the coral reefs offer a chance to witness a vibrant underwater world teeming with marine life, including sea turtles and colorful fish.

Okinawa Weather

Okinawa’s climate makes it a year-round destination, with each season offering its own unique charm. Whether you’re looking to bask in the summer sun or enjoy the milder winter weather, Okinawa’s subtropical climate ensures that there’s always something to enjoy.

Top 6 Local Specialties

Okinawa Soba

a beloved noodle dish that reflects the unique culinary heritage of Okinawa. Unlike traditional Japanese soba noodles made from buckwheat, Okinawa Soba features thicker wheat noodles served in a clear and savory broth made from pork, fish, and seaweed.

Rafute

a traditional Okinawan dish consisting of braised pork belly simmered in a sweet and savory sauce until tender and flavorful. The pork belly is typically cooked slowly with ingredients such as soy sauce, sake (Japanese rice wine), sugar, and sometimes dashi (Japanese soup stock) or awamori (Okinawan distilled liquor).

Goya Champuru

a popular stir-fry dish from Okinawa, known for its distinctive flavors and health benefits. It features bitter melon (goya), tofu, egg, and various ingredients such as pork, Spam, or other meats, stir-fried together in a savory sauce.

Taco Rice

It consists of seasoned ground beef (or other proteins like chicken or pork), tomatoes, lettuce, and cheese served on a bed of rice. The dish is typically topped with salsa, sour cream, and sometimes avocado to enhance its flavors.

Sata Andagi

a traditional Okinawan sweet treat resembling a doughnut or deep-fried bun. Made from flour, sugar, eggs, and baking powder, the dough is rolled into balls and deep-fried until golden brown.

Agu Pork

a prized variety of pork native to Okinawa, known for its exceptional flavor and quality. Raised on the island's lush pastures and fed a diet rich in local ingredients, Agu Pork is celebrated for its tender texture, marbling, and rich umami taste. It is often enjoyed in traditional Okinawan dishes such as Rafute (braised pork belly) or simply grilled to highlight its natural flavors.
