NAGOYA

Nagoya: Unveil the Hidden Treasures of Central Japan!

Nagoya, Japan's fourth-largest city, offers a captivating blend of historical landmarks, modern innovation, and culinary delights.

Home to iconic sites like Nagoya Castle and Atsuta Shrine, this vibrant metropolis also boasts futuristic architecture, including the landmark Nagoya TV Tower. Renowned for its unique local cuisine such as hitsumabushi (grilled eel over rice) and miso katsu (breaded pork cutlet with miso sauce), Nagoya invites visitors to explore its rich cultural heritage and dynamic urban landscape.

Nagoya Weather

Nagoya experiences a humid subtropical climate, characterized by hot, humid summers and mild winters. Nagoya's climate ensures a distinct experience throughout the year, making it a welcoming destination for travelers interested in exploring its cultural landmarks and diverse attractions.

Top 6 Local Specialties

Hitsumabushi

a traditional dish from Nagoya, Japan, consisting of grilled freshwater eel (unagi) served over a bowl of rice. The eel is typically grilled with a sweet soy-based sauce, imparting a rich and savory flavor.

Tebasaki

known for its flavorful and crispy chicken wings. The wings are typically seasoned with salt or a savory soy-based sauce before being deep-fried to achieve a golden-brown exterior and juicy interior.

Miso Katsu

Miso Katsu is a beloved dish from Nagoya, Japan, featuring a breaded and deep-fried pork cutlet (tonkatsu) smothered in a rich and savory miso-based sauce. The pork cutlet is crispy on the outside and tender on the inside, complemented by the umami flavors of the miso sauce.

Kishimen

Kishimen is a type of flat, wide noodle that originated in Nagoya, Japan. These noodles are similar to udon but thinner and flatter, resembling fettuccine in shape. Kishimen is typically served in a savory broth made from soy sauce and dashi, accompanied by various toppings such as green onions, kamaboko (fish cake), and sometimes tempura.

Tenmusu

a specialty dish originating from Nagoya, Japan, consisting of tempura shrimp (ten) wrapped in seasoned rice and wrapped in a layer of nori seaweed. This portable and satisfying snack combines the crispy texture of tempura shrimp with the comforting flavors of seasoned rice, making it a popular choice for lunch or a quick bite.

Ankake Spaghetti

Ankake Spaghetti is a unique and popular dish in Nagoya, Japan, blending Japanese and Italian culinary influences. It features spaghetti noodles topped with a thick and savory sauce (ankake) made from a combination of ingredients such as vegetables, seafood, and sometimes meat, thickened with starch.
