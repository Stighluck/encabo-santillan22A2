HOKKAIDO

Discover Hokkaido: Where Nature Unfolds in Every Season

Hokkaido, Japan's northern frontier, beckons with its vast wilderness and natural splendor, making it a haven for outdoor enthusiasts and urban escapists alike.

This island prefecture boasts stunning landscapes that change dramatically with each season: from fields of lavender and poppies in summer to snow-covered mountains perfect for skiing in winter. Explore the vibrant city life of Sapporo, known for its beer and snow festival, or unwind in the peaceful hot springs of Noboribetsu. Hokkaido's cuisine is equally enticing, featuring fresh seafood, renowned dairy products, and delectable ramen. Whether you're seeking adventure in nature, cultural experiences, or culinary delights, Hokkaido promises an unforgettable journey through its diverse and captivating offerings.

Hokkaido Weather

Hokkaido experiences a diverse climate with distinct seasons. Summers are generally mild and pleasant, ideal for exploring outdoor activities and festivals. Winters are cold and snowy, making it a prime destination for skiing and enjoying hot springs. Spring and autumn offer comfortable temperatures with vibrant cherry blossoms and colorful foliage, respectively, making Hokkaido a year-round destination with something unique to offer each season.

Top 6 Local Specialties

Soup Curry

This unique dish features a spicy, flavorful broth filled with various vegetables, meats, and sometimes seafood, served with a side of rice. Soup curry is a beloved local specialty in Hokkaido, and many restaurants in Sapporo are dedicated to perfecting their own versions of this hearty and delicious meal.

Jingisukan

Jingisukan is a type of Japanese barbecue where lamb or mutton is grilled on a dome-shaped metal skillet or grill. The meat is often marinated or seasoned before grilling and is served with a variety of vegetables.

Sashimi

The island's cold, nutrient-rich waters provide an ideal environment for a variety of seafood, making it a prime location for fresh and flavorful sashimi. Some of the most popular types of sashimi in Hokkaido include uni (sea urchin), ika (squid), hotate (scallops), and a variety of fresh fish like salmon and tuna.

Yubari Melon

This sweet and juicy melon, grown in the town of Yubari, is a prized fruit in Japan and is often given as a luxury gift.

Sapporo-style Ramen

Sapporo-style ramen is one of the most famous regional ramen varieties in Japan and has contributed significantly to Hokkaido's culinary reputation. Many ramen shops in Sapporo and across Japan serve this iconic dish, and it is a must-try for visitors to the region.

Zangi

A type of Japanese fried chicken. Zangi is similar to karaage, another popular Japanese fried chicken dish.
